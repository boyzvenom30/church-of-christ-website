Who We Are

Get to Know Us

The Church of Christ is a worldwide family of independent, non-denominational Christian congregations. We have no central headquarters, president, or governing board. Instead, we recognize **Jesus Christ** as the sole head of the church.

Our goal is simple: we want to follow the teachings of the Bible as closely as possible. We seek to restore the simplicity, worship, and practice of the first-century church as described in the New Testament. We strive to be **Christians only**, without wearing human-made names or labels.

Our Core Goal

Restoring New Testament Christianity

We do not view ourselves as a new denomination, nor do we seek to reform existing ones. Instead, we aim to bypass centuries of division by going straight back to the original source.

By following the Bible as our only guide, we speak where the Bible speaks, and remain silent where the Bible is silent.

WHAT WE BELIEVE

What We Believe & Teach

A clear, simple, and biblical overview of our doctrine and faith, following the New Testament pattern.

The New Testament Church

The Church of Christ is not a modern organization. It dates back to the days of the New Testament (Romans 16:16). It was founded by Jesus Christ on the Day of Pentecost in A.D. 33 (Acts 2) right after His ascension to Heaven. Since then, the church grew rapidly to reach all corners of the earth (Acts 1:8, Colossians 1:23).

One Unified Body

The Bible uses the words "church" and "body" interchangeably (Ephesians 1:22-23, Colossians 1:18, 24). Scriptures clearly teach there is only one body, not many (Ephesians 4:4, Romans 12:4-5, 1 Corinthians 12:13, 20). Jesus Christ is the sole head of this one body and holds all authority (Ephesians 5:23, Matthew 28:18, Ephesians 4:4-6).

Many Members, United

The one body is made of many individual Christian members, not different denominations (1 Corinthians 12:12-27, Acts 8:1-4). We are commanded to be united in faith and purpose (1 Corinthians 1:10-13, Romans 12:16). Division is sinful, and we must withdraw from divisive, disorderly, or immoral behavior (Romans 16:17, Titus 3:10, 1 Corinthians 5:1ff, 2 Thessalonians 3:16).

Our Primary Mission

Our main mission is to share the gospel with the entire world, just as Jesus commanded (Matthew 28:18-20, Mark 16:15-16). We strive to show the same love to one another that Christ showed to us, actively practicing kindness, charity, and benevolence to those in need (John 13:34, James 1:27, Acts 20:28, Galatians 6:10).

Universal Church and Local Church

The Bible talks about the church in two distinct senses. First, the Universal Church, which includes every saved person in the entire world. Second, the Local Church, which is a congregation of believers in a specific area. There is no central governing authority or headquarters; each congregation is independent (autonomous), following Jesus Christ as their only head, the Bible as their only law, and organized with elders, deacons, preachers, and members (Philippians 1:1, Acts 14:23, 1 Timothy 3:1-13, Titus 1:5-9, Ephesians 4:11-12, Romans 12:4-5).

Belonging to Christ

The description "church of Christ" simply denotes ownership—it is the church that belongs to Christ. The New Testament reveals that the church is built on Him, purchased by His blood, has the saved added to it, acts as the bride of Christ, and will ultimately be delivered up to God at the end (Matthew 16:18, Acts 20:28, 1 Corinthians 15:24).

Scriptural Truths

The Church in God's Word

  • ✔ Eternal Purpose: Part of God's eternal plan (Ephesians 3:9-11)
  • ✔ House of God: Living household of the living God (1 Timothy 3:15)
  • ✔ Built by Christ: Established upon His authority (Matthew 16:18-19)
  • ✔ Founded on Jesus: Christ is the only foundation (1 Corinthians 3:11)
  • ✔ Purchased: Bought with His own blood (Acts 20:28)
  • ✔ Added to: The Lord adds the saved to it daily (Acts 2:47)
  • ✔ Bride of Christ: Joined to Christ in covenant (Romans 7:1-4, Ephesians 5:22-23)
  • ✔ Delivered: Will be delivered to God in the end (1 Corinthians 15:24)
Worship

What to Expect When You Visit

If you are planning to attend our services, we want you to feel welcome and know what to expect during our assembly:

A Cappella Singing

In accordance with New Testament instructions, our congregational singing is vocal only (a cappella) without instrumental accompaniment. Every member joins in heartfelt vocal praise (Ephesians 5:19, Colossians 3:16).

Bible-Centered Teaching

Sermons and bible studies are centered entirely on the scriptures. We encourage everyone to follow along in their own Bibles to verify that the things taught align with God's word (Acts 17:11).

Weekly Communion

We observe the Lord's Supper (communion) every Sunday (the first day of the week), just as the early disciples did. It is a time of remembrance and reflection on the sacrifice of Christ (Acts 20:7, 1 Corinthians 11:23-28).

Free-Will Giving

A collection is made on Sundays for the work of the church. This is a free-will contribution by our members. Visitors are not expected or solicited to give money (1 Corinthians 16:1-2).

Outreach

Mission & Community Works

Active ministries demonstrating the love of Christ in Chennai and beyond.

Free Medical Camps

We sponsor quarterly general health checkup camps in Kumaran Nagar, partnering with Christian medical professionals to provide free basic healthcare, diagnostics, and counseling to underprivileged families.

Education Sponsorship

Helping local children stay in school by providing school supplies, books, and scholarship funds to low-income families in our community, fostering positive growth and mentorship.

Disaster Relief & Aid

In times of monsoonal flooding and severe storms in Chennai, the church acts as a localized supply hub, distributing food packets, dry rations, and clean drinking water to affected families.

Who We Are

Who are the Churches of Christ?

A non-denominational fellowship seeking to live out New Testament Christianity in the modern world.

Christ as the Head

Following Ephesians 1:22-23, we acknowledge Jesus Christ as the sole head of the church. We answer to no earthly headquarters, boards, or human leaders.

Our Doctrine

One Faith & One Bible

We believe the Word of God is our only guide in spiritual matters (Ephesians 4:3-6). We reject human-made creeds, shepherding our life by scriptures alone.

View Beliefs

Local Autonomy

Each local church operates independently and is self-governing. We are led locally by a plurality of qualified elders who shepherd our specific assembly.

Our Leadership

Christians Only

We wear no human-designed names or titles. We identify simply as Christians, honoring the name of Christ as referenced in Romans 16:16.

Biblical Pattern

Understanding the New Testament Model

Here are additional scriptural principles that distinguish the churches of Christ in identity, names, and practice.

Not a Building, but the People

In the Bible, the word "church" never refers to a brick-and-mortar building. It always refers to the assembly of believers. The scriptures describe the church as the **Body of Christ** (Colossians 1:18), the **Household of God** (Ephesians 2:19), and the **Temple of the Holy Spirit** (1 Corinthians 3:16).

Why We Wear the Name of Christ

We do not use "Church of Christ" as a brand or a denominational label. It is a scriptural description meaning "the assembly belonging to Christ." Since Jesus built the church and purchased it with His own blood, it is only natural that we wear His name (Matthew 16:18, Romans 16:16, Acts 20:28).

Elders, Preachers, and Deacons

Local congregations are organized according to the New Testament pattern. A plurality of qualified **Elders** (also called pastors or overseers) shepherd the local flock. **Deacons** are appointed to serve physical and administrative needs, while **Preachers** and evangelists share the gospel and teach the word (1 Timothy 3:1-13, Titus 1:5-9).

The Restoration Principle

Our commitment is to "speak where the Bible speaks, and remain silent where the Bible is silent." By letting the New Testament serve as our only guide, we aim to eliminate modern religious divisions and unite everyone under the simple authority of Jesus Christ (1 Peter 4:11, John 17:21).
